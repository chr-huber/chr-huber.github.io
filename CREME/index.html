Crowd Research in Behavioral Economics MEthods

With CREME we are running the first crowd-sourced expermient in behavioral economics!

In research teams (RTs) of two, everyone – from PhD student to full professor from around the world – will have the possibility to propose an experimental research design, all having with the aim to assess the same research question:

How does competition affect moral behavior?

We will solicit as many research designs as possible from the relevant community (i.e., from you) fulfilling a certain set of simple criteria. Learn more about the participation criteria and tasks for the research teams.

We will then study the variation in design choices and results, and write a meta science paper on the topic where all contributors whose proposals were run are also co-authors.

procedure

After successful application, there are five stages in CREME: submission of design proposals, implementation of the experiment, peer evaluation, data analysis, and writing of the paper.

  1. Application procedure
    1. Research teams (RTs) apply for taking part in this study (without yet submitting a research design)
    2. Project coordinators (PCs) pre-screen applications for accordance with the researcher conditions and invite all eligible RTs to submit a design proposal.
  2. Design submission and selection procedure
    1. Invited RTs submit their experimental research design proposal.
    2. PCs pre-screen all submitted designs for accordance with the design conditions.
    3. If more than 40 design proposals are eligible, 40 designs will be randomly selected for being included in the study.
  3. Experimental implementation
    • All 40 selected designs will be implemented as online experiments using Prolific, payed for by the PCs.
    • All 40 selected RTs program the experimental software for their design proposal themselves.
    • All 40 selected RTs host the experimental software for their design proposal on their own servers.
  4. Peer evaluation
  5. Analysis
    • RTs will analyze the data as specified in their pre-analysis plan.
    • RTs will report their main treatment effect (in terms of the effect size and the corresponding standard error).
    • RTs will submit their raw data and their analysis scripts.
    • PCs will do additional, own analyses as specified in their pre-analysis plan.
  6. PCs will write a meta science paper; all 40 selected RTs will be co-authors.

Project coordinators. The project will be coordinated by researchers from the Stockholm School of Economics, the University of Innsbruck, and the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. Meet the project coordinators.
