Welcome to my website!

I am a research assistant and PhD candidate at the Department of Banking and Finance at the University of Innsbruck and doing research in Behavioural and Experimental Finance and Economics.

Teaching

After graduating from a science gymnasium in Innsbruck I started my studies of economics and business administration at the FH Kufstein Tirol (University of Applied Sciences) with a one-year stay at the Dublin Institute of Technology, where I specialised in economics and finance. After graduating in 2014 I started the master's program Applied Economics as well as the master's program Banking and Finance at the University of Innsbruck, from which I graduated in 2016.

New Since October 2016, I have been enrolled in the joint PhD Program in Economics of the University of Innsbruck and the Johannes-Kepler-University Linz.

From March to June 2016 I worked as a student research assistant at the Department of Banking and Finance at the University of Innsbruck and – after gaining a PhD grant from the Netzwerk Banking, Auditing, Finance & IT (University of Innsbruck) – continued my research in the Behavioural and Experimental Finance team of Jürgen Huber and Michael Kirchler through February 2017.

New Since March 2017 I have been a research assistant at the Department of Banking and Finance at the University of Innsbruck.

My research focuses on Behavioural and Experimental Economics and Finance, Decision Theory, Market Efficiency and Bubbles, and Information Economics.
